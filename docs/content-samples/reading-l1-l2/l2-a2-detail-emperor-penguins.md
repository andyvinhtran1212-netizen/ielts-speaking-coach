# Emperor Penguins in Winter

Emperor penguins live in Antarctica, where the winter is the harshest on Earth. Temperatures can fall far below zero, and strong winds make it even colder.

To keep warm, thousands of penguins **huddle** close together in a large group. The birds on the outside are the coldest, so they slowly move inwards. By taking turns, every penguin gets a warm place near the centre.

Emperor penguins also share their work. The father holds the egg on his feet to keep it warm while the mother travels to the sea to find food. When she returns, the parents **take turns** again so both can eat and rest.
