# How Rainbows Form

Have you ever seen seven bright colours in the sky after the rain? That is a **rainbow**. A rainbow needs two things to **form**: rain and sun. So you often see one when the sun comes out during a light shower.

A rainbow is made from **sunlight**. Sunlight looks white, but it is really made of many colours. When sunlight goes into a tiny water **drop**, the light **bends**. Each **colour** bends a little differently. Because of this, the white light **splits** into seven colours. They go from red at the top to violet at the bottom.

To see a rainbow, you must stand in the right place. The sun must be behind you, and the rain must be in front of you. Then the colours form a big **arch** across the sky.

You can even make your own rainbow at home. On a sunny day, turn on a **garden hose** and make a fine **spray**. With the sun behind you, the colours may appear in the water.
