# How Rainbows Form

A rainbow appears when sunlight passes through tiny drops of water in the air.

White light is made of many colours. When light enters a **droplet**, it **bends** and **separates** into different colours. Each colour bends at a slightly different angle, so we see them as a band of colours: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and violet.

You can only see a rainbow when the sun is behind you and the rain is in front of you. That is why rainbows often appear after a rain shower, when the sun comes out again.
