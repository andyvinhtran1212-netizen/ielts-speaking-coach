# Section 1 Deep Dive — Cookery class enrolment

**Section type:** polite-transactional | **Words:** 708 | **Section ID:** S1

## Context Primer / Bối cảnh

**Setting:** Phone enquiry about cookery class enrolment at the Riverside Cookery School

In the UK and Australia, **adult cookery schools** (trường dạy nấu ăn cho người lớn) usually offer multi-week courses tiered by ability: *absolute beginner* → *beginner* → *intermediate* → *advanced*. A typical enquiry call captures **address + postcode** (UK postcodes are alphanumeric, e.g. BN1 6QR), **mobile number** (UK mobiles begin with **07…**), preferred **day/time slot**, and an **early-bird discount** (thường giảm 10% nếu trả trước hạn). Learners should expect food-skills vocabulary like *knife skills*, *sauces*, *pastry* (đồ làm bánh), *baking techniques*, plus enrolment terms such as *apron* (tạp dề), *deposit*, *cancellation policy* và *cap class size* (giới hạn sĩ số).

# Common Trap Patterns Across This Test

Trap mechanisms appearing trong test này (sắp xếp theo tần suất):

| Trap mechanism | Count | Vietnamese explanation |
|---|---|---|
| `paraphrase_t1` | 20 | Paraphrase tier 1 — đổi 1 cụm với synonym |

| `paraphrase_t2` | 6 | Paraphrase tier 2 — restructure phrase |
| `polarity_flip` | 6 | Đảo cực — "not X but Y" / phủ định chuyển thành khẳng định |
| `number_correction` | 4 | Số sửa lại — số ban đầu bị sửa thành số khác |

| `definition_vs_example` | 4 | Định nghĩa vs ví dụ — term được nói chung rồi liệt kê ví dụ |
| `paraphrase_t3` | 2 | Paraphrase tier 3 — chuyển nghĩa qua nhiều bước |
| `self_correction` | 1 | Tự sửa — speaker nói X rồi đổi "actually..." |
| `name_spelling` | 1 | Tên đánh vần — spelled letter-by-letter |

**Total distinct mechanisms:** 11 (out of 19 canonical types)

### Trap "killer combos" — what made this test challenging

1. **Polarity-flip stacks (Q4, Q11, Q13, Q14, Q15)** — repeated "not X but Y" sentences in S2 MCQ. The pivot signals — *but, however, instead, rather than, not just* — must be tracked in real time.
2. **Map double-constraint (Q16–Q19)** — every map question used ≥ 2 directional cues (left + outside wall + larger). Single-cue listeners over-pick the wrong room.
3. **Definition vs example (Q9, Q33, Q36)** — ingredients listed (flour/eggs/butter), gas networks (gas works/pipes), light pollution (stars/birds/bedrooms). Candidates who write a listed example lose the mark.
4. **Tier-3 inferential paraphrase (Q39, Q40)** — "colour temperature → sleep" and "data collection → surveillance" require one inferential step. These are the hardest items for band 5.5 — usually missed by lower-band candidates.

## Connected speech & pronunciation notes

### Connected speech & pronunciation patterns to train your ear

These features appear in the audio at band 5.5 — recognising them is essential for catching keywords.

1. **"I'll" /aɪl/** — contracted form of *I will*. In S3, Priya says "**I'll** do recommendations too" (Q26). Train your ear: the contraction is fast and easy to miss, but signals a future commitment, not a content word.
2. **"sort of" /sɔːt əv/** — vague hedge. Priya in S3: "I find that part, **sort of**, interesting." The *of* reduces to a schwa /əv/ in connected speech — common in informal hedging.
3. **"reckon" /ˈrek.ən/** — AusE/BrE casual verb meaning *think* or *believe*. Daniel (AusE) in S1: "So it'd have to be **Saturday** morning, I **reckon**." Highly characteristic of AusE.
4. **"mate" /meɪt/** — AusE/BrE term of address, like "buddy". A student in S3: "Yeah good point, **mate**." A speech marker — never content.
5. **Self-correction signals** — *Sorry, hang on… actually… let me check…* — listen for these as cues that a NEW answer is coming (Q2: BN1 6QP → BN1 6QR; Q3: 423 → 432).
6. **Schwa reductions in function words** — *for* /fə/, *to* /tə/, *and* /ən/, *of* /əv/ in rapid speech. E.g. Helen: "Week one is knife skills" → "Week-one-is" runs together.
7. **Linking "r"** in BrE — Helen: "a *ratio of* six **to** one" — the *o* of "ratio" links onto the next word.

### Hard pronunciations to watch (BrE / AusE)

- **postcode** /ˈpəʊst.kəʊd/ — two clean syllables; BrE *post* is /pəʊst/, never /poʊst/ (AmE).
- **ingredients** /ɪnˈɡriː.di.ənts/ — four syllables; stress on "gree". Common error: dropping the final /ts/.
- **pastry** /ˈpeɪ.stri/ — NOT /ˈpæs.tri/ ("pasty" is a different word — savoury meat pie).
- **questionnaire** /ˌkwes.tʃəˈneə/ — French-origin; stress on FINAL syllable (-naire).
- **surveillance** /səˈveɪ.ləns/ — three syllables; stress on "vei"; the -ll- is single /l/.
- **distorted** /dɪˈstɔː.tɪd/ — past participle as adjective; the -ed = /ɪd/ because the stem ends in /t/.
- **astronomer** /əˈstrɒn.ə.mə/ — stress on "stron"; final /-ə/ in BrE (non-rhotic), not /-ər/.
