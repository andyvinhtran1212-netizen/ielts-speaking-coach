| Loại | Ví dụ | Độ không chắc |
|------|-------|---------------|
| **Modal verbs** | might / could / may | Trung bình |
| **Adverbs** | probably / possibly / perhaps / presumably | Trung bình |
| **Epistemic phrases** | I think / I believe / I suspect / I imagine | Nhẹ đến trung bình |
| **Hedging phrases** | It seems / It appears / It's possible that | Trung bình đến mạnh |
| **Distancing** | Tend to / It could be argued | Rất thận trọng |

## Tại sao quan trọng

Người học thường mắc hai thái cực:
1. **Khẳng định quá mức**: "Technology will destroy jobs." *(quá tuyệt đối)*
2. **Không dám nói**: "I don't know. It's hard to say." *(quá ngắn, không có nội dung)*

Band 7+ cần thể hiện khả năng **phân tích có sắc thái** — đưa ra quan điểm trong khi thừa nhận sự phức tạp. Đây chính là lúc hedging language phát huy tác dụng.

## PHẦN 1: MODAL VERBS — Nền tảng của sự không chắc chắn

### might / could / may:

Ba modal verbs này đều diễn đạt khả năng — nhưng với sắc thái khác nhau:

| Modal | Sắc thái | Ví dụ |
|-------|---------|-------|
| **may** | Khả năng chung (neutral) | This **may** lead to unemployment. |
| **might** | Ít chắc hơn "may" | The policy **might** have unintended consequences. |
| **could** | Khả năng trong số nhiều khả năng | Remote work **could** become the norm. |

### Dùng trong Part 3:

> Q: "Do you think traditional jobs will disappear in the future?"
> "Some jobs **might** disappear as automation advances, but I think new types of work will also emerge. It's **possible** that roles requiring human creativity and emotional intelligence **could** become even more valuable."

### would — Giả thuyết và suy đoán có điều kiện:

- "Better education **would** reduce inequality, at least in theory."
- "If governments invested more, things **would** likely improve."
- "I **would imagine** that most people feel this way."

## PHẦN 2: ADVERBS — Đánh dấu mức độ chắc chắn

```
[Subject] + probably/possibly/perhaps/presumably + [verb phrase]
Perhaps/Possibly, [full sentence].
```

| Trạng từ | Mức độ chắc chắn | Ví dụ |
|----------|-----------------|-------|
| **certainly / definitely** | Rất chắc (~90%+) | This will **certainly** continue. |
| **probably** | Khá chắc (~70%) | This will **probably** continue. |
| **likely** | Khá chắc | Change is **likely** to happen. |
| **possibly / perhaps** | Không chắc (~50%) | This **might possibly** cause issues. |
| **presumably** | Suy đoán từ bằng chứng | People are **presumably** more stressed. |
| **conceivably** | Chỉ là lý thuyết | This **could conceivably** work. |

### Trong câu trả lời:

- "Technology will **probably** continue to change the workplace, though it's hard to predict the exact extent."
- "**Perhaps** the most important factor is education — if people are better informed, they tend to make better decisions."
- "Younger generations are **likely** more comfortable with change than older ones, though this is a generalisation."

## PHẦN 3: CÁC CỤM EPISTEMIC — Thể hiện quan điểm có đánh dấu

Những cụm này báo hiệu rằng đây là **ý kiến của người nói**, không phải sự thật tuyệt đối:

### Nhẹ nhàng (I think / I believe):

- "**I think** this is gradually changing."
- "**I believe** remote work has long-term benefits, though I'm not certain."
- "**I imagine** most people would agree, at least to some extent."

### Thận trọng hơn:

- "**I suspect** the situation is more complex than it first appears."
- "**I would guess** that social media plays a significant role, though it's hard to measure."
- "**I get the impression that** younger people are more optimistic about this."

### Mạnh hơn (nhấn vào sự không chắc):

- "**It's hard to say** for certain, but I think..."
- "**I'm not entirely sure**, but it seems as though..."
- "**I can't say for certain**, though my feeling is that..."

---

## PHẦN 4: IMPERSONAL HEDGING — Khách quan hóa sự không chắc

Thay vì nói "I think maybe...", bạn có thể dùng cấu trúc vô nhân xưng để nghe trang trọng và khách quan hơn:

### It seems / It appears / It looks as though:

- "**It seems** that many young people are delaying home ownership."
- "**It appears** that remote work has both benefits and drawbacks."
- "**It looks as though** the trend will continue for the foreseeable future."

### There is/are + uncertain noun phrases:

- "**There's a possibility** that technology could replace many manual jobs."
- "**There's reason to believe** that education improves social mobility."
- "**There's some evidence to suggest** that social media affects mental health."

### Tend to (generalising without absolute claim):

- "People **tend to** underestimate the time needed."
- "Students **tend to** do better when they have a clear routine."
- "It **tends to** depend on the individual."

---

## PHẦN 5: DISTANCING — Nói về quan điểm của người khác

Khi bạn không chắc hoặc muốn báo cáo một quan điểm mà không hoàn toàn cam kết:

- "**Some people would argue** that social media has done more harm than good."
- "**It could be argued** that this policy has had unintended consequences."
- "**Many experts believe** this trend will continue, though there's debate on the extent."
- "**It is sometimes claimed** that technology makes people less social — though I'm not sure I entirely agree."

## PHẦN 6: KẾT HỢP — Câu trả lời Part 3 hoàn chỉnh

### Mẫu: Acknowledge uncertainty + give view + reason + qualify

```
[Uncertainty signal] + [your view]. [Reason/evidence]. [Qualifying remark].
```

> Q: "Do you think social media has had a positive or negative effect on society?"
> "**That's a really difficult question** — I think the answer **probably** depends on how it's used. **It seems to me that** platforms like Instagram and TikTok **can** have a genuinely negative impact on self-esteem, particularly for younger users. **That said**, social media **might** also be a force for good in terms of connecting communities and spreading awareness. **I suspect** the long-term effects are something researchers are **still trying to** fully understand."

> Q: "What do you think will happen to traditional retail in the future?"
> "**It's hard to say** with any certainty, but **I would imagine** that physical shops will **probably** continue to decline, especially those that **don't** adapt. **Presumably**, shops that offer an experience — not just products — **will** survive. But **there's also a possibility** that people **might** value in-person shopping more after years of buying everything online. **I'm genuinely not sure** which way it'll go."

## Lỗi thường gặp

### Lỗi 1: Nói "I don't know" và dừng lại

- ❌ "I don't know. It's difficult."
- ✅ "**That's a difficult one.** I think **it's possible that**... **though I'm not entirely sure** about the extent."

### Lỗi 2: Khẳng định quá mức — không có hedge

- ❌ "Technology will destroy all jobs."
- ✅ "Technology **might** significantly reduce the number of certain jobs, **though it's likely** that new roles will also emerge."

### Lỗi 3: Quá nhiều "I think" (xem bài overusing-i-think)

- ❌ "I think... I think... I think..."
- ✅ "I think... **It seems to me**... **I would imagine**... **There's reason to believe**..."

### Lỗi 4: "Maybe" quá nhiều lần liên tiếp

- ❌ "Maybe it's good. Maybe it's bad. Maybe the government should do something."
- ✅ "The situation **is probably** mixed — **it seems** beneficial in some ways, but **there's also reason** to think it causes harm. **Perhaps** the most effective response would be at the policy level."

## Tóm tắt nhanh

```
MODAL VERBS (khả năng):
  might / could / may → "This might change."
  would → "This would probably improve things."

ADVERBS (mức độ chắc chắn):
  probably → fairly sure
  possibly / perhaps → less sure
  presumably → inferred from evidence
  likely → probable

EPISTEMIC PHRASES:
  I think / I believe → light uncertainty
  I suspect / I imagine / I get the impression → moderate
  I'm not entirely sure, but... → honest hedging

IMPERSONAL HEDGING:
  It seems that / It appears that
  There's a possibility that / There's reason to believe
  It tends to... / It could be argued that...

DISTANCING:
  Some would argue... / It could be argued...
  Many experts believe... / It is sometimes claimed...

IELTS PART 3 PATTERN:
  Hard claim → Hedge → Reason → Qualification
  "Technology might reduce jobs, but new roles will likely emerge."
```
