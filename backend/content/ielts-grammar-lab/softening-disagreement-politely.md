## Tại sao quan trọng

Trong IELTS Speaking Part 3, examiner thường nói những câu như:
- "Some people think that technology is ruining communication. What do you think?"
- "Do you agree that young people today are less hardworking than previous generations?"

Đây là cơ hội để **thể hiện khả năng lập luận có chiều sâu**. Phản đối lịch sự không có nghĩa là thiếu tự tin — ngược lại, nó cho thấy bạn có tư duy phân tích và biết giao tiếp khéo léo.

---

## PHẦN 1: ACKNOWLEDGE BEFORE COUNTERING — Ghi nhận trước, phản đối sau

Kỹ thuật quan trọng nhất: **thừa nhận có giá trị trong quan điểm kia**, rồi mới đưa ra phản bác. Điều này khiến bạn nghe thuyết phục hơn nhiều so với phản đối thẳng.

### Cấu trúc:

```
[Acknowledge the view] + [counter/qualification].
```

### Cụm thừa nhận:

| Cụm | Sắc thái |
|-----|---------|
| **That's a fair point, but...** | Trung lập, lịch sự |
| **I can see where that's coming from, but...** | Thấu hiểu quan điểm đối lập |
| **There's certainly some truth in that, though...** | Thừa nhận một phần |
| **I understand why people might think that, however...** | Đồng cảm nhưng không đồng ý |
| **That's one way of looking at it, but I think...** | Mở ra góc nhìn khác |
| **You could argue that, but on the other hand...** | Thừa nhận là lập luận hợp lệ |

### Ví dụ:

> Examiner: "Some people say social media is destroying real human connection."
> "**That's a fair point**, and I can see why people feel that way. **However**, I think it's a bit more nuanced than that — for people who live far from family, social media can actually strengthen connections rather than weaken them."

> Examiner: "Do you think universities are becoming less valuable?"
> "**I can see where that's coming from** — the cost of education is rising while job prospects aren't always guaranteed. **That said**, I'd argue that university still offers something beyond just job training: the critical thinking and social development are hard to replicate."

## PHẦN 2: PARTIAL AGREEMENT — Đồng ý một phần

Khi bạn không hoàn toàn đồng ý và không hoàn toàn phản đối — câu trả lời thực tế nhất và cũng thể hiện tư duy tốt nhất.

### Cấu trúc:

```
To some extent, [agreement]. However / But [qualification].
[Agreement side]. [Limitation or counter].
```

### Cụm partial agreement:

- "**To some extent**, I agree — but it's not the whole picture."
- "**I think there's something to that**, though it depends on the context."
- "**In some cases**, that may be true. **But** I think it's a generalisation."
- "**You're right that** [X] is a problem. **But** I wouldn't say it's the only factor."
- "**That's partly true**, though I'd add that..."

### Ví dụ:

> Examiner: "Do you think people are too dependent on smartphones?"
> "**To some extent**, yes — I think many people, myself included, probably spend more time on their phones than is ideal. **But** I'd stop short of saying it's a dependency problem for most people. It's more that smartphones have become genuinely necessary for daily life — navigation, banking, communication. **The issue**, I think, is about balance."

## PHẦN 3: SOFT MODAL VERBS — Không đồng ý mà không đối đầu

Sử dụng modal verbs để "hạ nhiệt" sự phản đối:

### Cấu trúc:

```
I'm not sure (that) I would / could / agree with...
I wouldn't say (that)...
I'm not entirely convinced that...
```

### Ví dụ:

- "**I'm not sure I'd go that far** — I think it's more complicated."
- "**I wouldn't say** technology is ruining society; it's more that we're still adapting to it."
- "**I'm not entirely convinced** that stricter laws are the solution — education might be more effective."
- "I **find it hard to agree completely** with that view, mainly because..."
- "I **wouldn't necessarily agree** — it really depends on the situation."
- "**I'm not sure that's always true**, though I can see why people might think so."

## PHẦN 4: DISTANCING AND REFRAMING — Chuyển hướng khéo léo

### Impersonal distancing (không phải "tôi không đồng ý" mà "lập luận đó có vấn đề"):

- "**It could be argued** that this overlooks a key point: [your counter]."
- "**The issue with that argument** is that it assumes [X], which isn't always the case."
- "**That reasoning tends to ignore** the fact that [counter]."
- "**I think that view may be oversimplifying** a fairly complex issue."

### Reframing — Thay đổi góc nhìn:

- "**I think it's less a question of** [their frame] **and more about** [your frame]."
- "**Rather than** asking whether [X] is good or bad, **I think the real question is** [Y]."
- "**I'd frame it slightly differently** — it's not so much [their claim] as [your view]."

### Ví dụ:

> Examiner: "Some argue that governments should ban junk food advertising."
> "**I think it's less a question of** banning and more about **finding the right level of regulation**. A complete ban **might** seem heavy-handed, but **I think the real issue** is that children are particularly susceptible to advertising — **so perhaps** the focus should be on restricting advertising during children's programming rather than banning it outright."

## PHẦN 5: MẪU CÂU TRẢ LỜI HOÀN CHỈNH

### Mẫu A — Acknowledge + Partial disagree + Reason + Alternative view:

```
[Acknowledge] + [soft disagreement] + [reason] + [alternative].
```

> Q: "Do you think learning a foreign language in school is a waste of time for some students?"
> "**I understand why some might think that**, especially if students never end up using the language. **But I'm not sure I'd agree** with the idea that it's a waste of time. **Even if** someone never becomes fluent, learning another language **tends to** develop problem-solving skills and cultural awareness. **I think the real issue** is more about how languages are taught — if it's purely grammar and tests, students won't see the value."

### Mẫu B — Grant concession + Counter + Reframe:

```
[Grant that X is true]. [However, counter]. [Reframe the issue].
```

> Q: "Some people say older workers can't adapt to new technology."
> "**There's certainly some truth in the idea** that younger people often pick up new technology faster. **That said, I'd push back on the generalisation** that older workers can't adapt — in my experience, it's often a question of opportunity and training rather than ability. **I think the real issue** is that companies invest less in training older employees, rather than any inherent limitation."

## PHẦN 6: CHUYỂN TIẾP SAU KHI PHẢN ĐỐI

Sau khi đưa ra phản đối, luôn phát triển ý hoặc đưa bằng chứng:

- "...and **the reason I say this** is that..."
- "...which is supported by the fact that..."
- "...**I think what's more likely** is..."
- "...**a better explanation might be**..."
- "...**and from what I've seen**, this is actually what happens in practice."

## Lỗi thường gặp

### Lỗi 1: Đồng ý hoàn toàn để tránh khó

- ❌ "Yes, I agree completely." *(mất cơ hội thể hiện tư duy)*
- ✅ "**To some extent**, yes. **But** I think there's another side to this..."

### Lỗi 2: Phản đối thô lỗ hoặc quá trực tiếp

- ❌ "No, that's wrong." / "I totally disagree."
- ✅ "**I can see the argument**, but **I'd suggest** that it's more complicated than that."
- ✅ "**I'm not sure I'd agree entirely** — I think [nuanced view]."

### Lỗi 3: Phản đối mà không giải thích tại sao

- ❌ "I don't think that's right."
- ✅ "I'm not sure that's entirely accurate, **mainly because**... / **the reason being that**..."

### Lỗi 4: Mở đầu bằng "Actually" (nghe thách thức)

- ❌ "**Actually**, that's not really true."
- ✅ "**I think there's a different way of looking at that** — actually, some research suggests..."
  
  *(Nếu dùng "actually", đặt nó ở giữa câu, không phải đầu câu)*
